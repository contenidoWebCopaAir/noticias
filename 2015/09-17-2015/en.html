Copa Airlines begins daily nonstop service between Panama and San Francisco

Copa Airlines today began daily nonstop flights to San Francisco, Calif., from the airline’s Hub of the Americas in Panama City, Panama.

San Francisco is Copa’s 12th destination in the U.S. and 74th overall.

San Francisco is one of the largest U.S. cities and a major city for tourism.

Panama City, Panama, Sept. 17, 2015

Copa Airlines {NYSE: CPA} subsidiary of Copa Holdings, S.A., and member of the Star Alliance global airline network, today began daily nonstop flights to San Francisco from Copa’s Hub of the Americas at Tocumen International Airport in Panama City, Panama.

The city of San Francisco is the main business, cultural and financial center of Northern California and also an important connection point between the United States, Asia and Oceania. Copa Airlines’ new nonstop flight opens up enormous opportunities for tourism and trade for Panama and the Latin American region in general. San Francisco also is the main West Coast U.S. hub for United Airlines; customers will be able to connect to/from nearby destinations such as Seattle, Wash., Portland, Ore., and Vancouver, Canada.

"Our new daily nonstop service to San Francisco demonstrates the Hub of the Americas’ continued leadership position among airline hubs in Latin America,” said Pablo de la Guardia, Director of Commercial Alliances and Governmental Relations, Copa Airlines. “San Francisco is our 12th U.S. destination and our 74th on the American continent. The opening of this route will enhance tourism and economic development between California, Panama and the rest of the region.”

Copa flight CM 208 departs the Hub of the Americas at Tocumen International Airport in Panama daily at 9:41 a.m., arriving at San Francisco International Airport at 2:54 p.m. The return flight, CM 209, departs San Francisco daily at 11:19 p.m., arriving in Panama at 8:32 a.m. the following day. Flight time is approximately 7 hours and 13 minutes. Copa is operating a Boeing 737-800 Next Generation aircraft on the approximately 6-hour, 45-minute flight, with Premium configuration and seating capacity for 16 passengers in Business Class and 138 in the main cabin.
